# Process Scheduling

**Process scheduling** is an OS task that schedules processes of different states like ready, waiting, and running.

## Preemptive vs Non-Preemptive
- **Preemptive Scheduling**: A process can be interrupted in the midst of its execution, taking the CPU away and allocating it to another process.
- **Non-Preemptive Scheduling**: Once the CPU has been allocated to a process, it keeps the CPU until it either terminates or blocks itself.

## Algorithms
- First Come First Serve (FCFS)
- Shortest Job First (SJF)
- Round Robin (RR)
- Priority Scheduling
